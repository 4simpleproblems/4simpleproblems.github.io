The Story of 4SP

4SP (4SimpleProblems) began as a small, personal project built with curiosity and passion. Initially released as Version 1 on March 17, 2025, the site featured basic functionality, a muted theme, and pill-shaped UI elements that reflected its experimental nature.

Encouraged by early feedback and driven by a desire to improve, Version 2 launched on April 11, 2025. This iteration kept the pill-shaped design but moved to a stark black-and-white color scheme. It was more refined and better structured, yet still experimental in many ways.

With increasing popularity and a growing user base, Version 3 debuted on May 14, 2025. It introduced a fully reimagined aesthetic—square corners, grid layouts, and a professional design focused on clarity and usability. Pages like the soundboard, games, notes, and dashboard all became staples of the platform.

By June and July, 4SP had evolved into a full ecosystem. A new sidebar unified navigation across the site, and systems like cloud/local notes, countdowns, GBA game emulators, playlist import/export, and proxy tools were all integrated. Every detail was optimized—from username editing to panic key shortcuts.

Now, 4SP is working toward Version 4, scheduled to release on August 23, 2025. This version will be the most comprehensive yet, with a live dashboard, editing tools, seamless linking, and complete cloaking features for its proxies. It reflects not only technical growth, but a deep understanding of what users want: control, customization, and connection.

4SP continues to be a testament to iterative design, user feedback, and one creator's dedication to building something that just works—and looks good doing it.
